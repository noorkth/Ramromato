About Us

Pioneering Sustainable Built Environment in Nepal

Ramro Mato, meaning "Good Mud," is a private firm dedicated to sustainable built environment practices through design and empirical solutions. Formed in 2018, we are a multidisciplinary team of architects, engineers, and builders specialising in sustainable building design and construction.

Our vision — Earth Enriched Environment — is a state where earth ameliorates and the good earth paradigm provides for sustained well-being. We promote the application of different forms of earth as building construction material and actively participate in sustainable built environment practices across Nepal.

Bagaicha Adventure Resort

Bagaicha Adventure Resort promotes cultural and adventure tourism while embracing the site’s natural setting, which includes fruit trees and landscaped gardens. The development maintains a symbiotic relationship with nature. Covering an area of 1.61 hectares, the site extends in a north–south direction. The planning approach emphasizes minimal intervention to existing site conditions, resulting in a layout where built structures merge seamlessly with the natural environment, similar to elements within a forest. The development consists of five main components: the entrance gate, arrival block, bar, banquet hall, and accommodation units. Each component is designed to blend with its surroundings while maintaining a clear functional distinction between spaces. The entrance gate serves as a landmark through its scale and ornamental features, while the arrival block represents hospitality by providing parking and essential services. As the site extends southward, it widens at the extremities, where an array of trees enhances the landscape and integrates harmoniously with the built structures.

The layout creates distinct private zones for each family while maintaining shared spaces that encourage community and interaction. The design emphasizes natural light, ventilation, and a connection to the outdoors, resulting in a home that is both functional and culturally rooted.

HimalayaAgro Cold Storage, Kagbeni

An apple and potato cold-storage facility built with locally sourced rammed earth in the high-altitude landscape of Mustang. Designed to harness the natural thermal mass of rammed earth walls — 2,014 cubic feet of compacted material — for passive temperature regulation, preserving agricultural produce without mechanical refrigeration.
